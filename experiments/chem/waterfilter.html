🔬 This Experiment
What we are doing: Simulating a water treatment plant and measuring final water turbidity (NTU).
Our aim: Find out which treatment parameters most affect final water quality.
What we will find: An AI model that predicts final NTU — and compare it to the textbook formula.
☕
Think of making tea. You are doing filtration — the same chemistry used at city scale. The leaves (solids) stay in the strainer; the liquid passes through. Water treatment is exactly this, with millions of litres per day.
The Textbook Formula
FINAL TURBIDITY AFTER TREATMENT
T_out = T_in × (1−E_coag) × (1−E_filt) × (1−E_UV)
T_in = raw turbidity (NTU)E = stage efficiencyGoal: ≤ 1 NTU
⚗️
The textbook formula assumes fixed efficiencies — but in reality, pH, temperature, coagulant dose, and organic load all interact. That's exactly what AI will learn from data that textbooks can't capture.
Now it is your turn. In the next steps you will work as a water chemist — collecting treatment data, training an AI model, and comparing what the AI discovers to the textbook formula.

🔬 Why We Collect Data
Now we need evidence — real numbers!
Parameters alone are just words. To teach an AI, we need measurements. Each data point is a trial: "with these treatment settings, final turbidity was this NTU."
Your job: Adjust the sliders and record turbidity values. The more varied your data, the smarter the AI becomes at predicting water quality.
🤖 After Data Collection
Then the AI takes over!
Once you have enough measurements, you'll pick an AI model to train. It will study your data and try to discover the hidden formula on its own — no textbook allowed!
The big reveal: We'll then compare what the AI found vs. what the textbook formula predicts. Spoiler: the AI should outperform fixed efficiencies. 🎯
