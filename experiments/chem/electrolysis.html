Many everyday items like jewelry are coated using electricity. In electroplating, metal ions move through a solution and deposit onto a surface, forming a shiny layer.
💍 Used in gold-plated jewelry and decorative coatings.
⚡ Driven by electric current — more current = faster plating.
🛠️ Used in electronics, tools, and corrosion protection.

🔬 This Experiment
What we are doing: Passing electricity through a solution and measuring how much substance is produced.
Our aim: Find out how voltage, time, and concentration control the amount deposited.
What we will find: A formula linking current, time, and Faraday's constant to moles produced.
🚰
Think of water flowing through a pipe. More pressure (voltage) = more flow (current). A wider pipe (lower resistance) lets more through. The total water delivered depends on both how fast it flows and how long you run it. Electrolysis works the same way — ions are the water.
The Formula
MOLES OF SUBSTANCE PRODUCED
n = (I · t) / (zF)
n = molesI = current (A)t = time (s)z = charge numberF = 96500 C/mol
🔋
Faraday's constant F = 96,485 C/mol is the charge of one mole of electrons. It means to deposit 1 mole of copper (z=2) you need 2 × 96,485 = 192,970 coulombs of charge. Faraday figured this out in 1834 with no knowledge of atoms!
