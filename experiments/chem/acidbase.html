🔬 This Experiment
What we are doing: Mixing acids and bases and measuring the resulting pH of the solution.
Our aim: Find out what controls the final pH — concentration, volume, or buffer strength.
What we will find: A formula that predicts pH from acid and base concentrations with great accuracy.
🌡️
Like a color thermometer for chemicals. Just as a thermometer shows temperature by color, an indicator dye shows pH by changing color. The more acid or base you add, the stronger the shift.
The Formula
pH FROM ACID–BASE MIXING
pH = −log₁₀[H⁺]
excess acid → [H⁺] = mol/Lexcess base → pOH = −log[OH⁻]neutralisation: pH = 7
📏
The pH scale is logarithmic — each step is a 10× change in acidity. Lemon juice (pH 2) is 100 000× more acidic than pure water (pH 7)!
Now it is your turn. In the next steps you will work as a chemist — collecting real pH measurements, training an AI model, and comparing what the AI finds to what the formula predicts.

Now we need evidence — real numbers!
Parameters alone are just words. To teach an AI, we need measurements. Each data point is a trial: "with these concentrations and volume, the pH was this value."
Your job: Adjust the sliders and record pH readings. The more varied your data, the smarter the AI becomes.
