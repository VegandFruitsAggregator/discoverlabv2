🔬 This Experiment
What we are doing: Dipping a paper strip in solvent and measuring how far components travel.
Our aim: Find out what controls how far each component separates.
What we will find: A formula using Rf that predicts separation with great accuracy.
🏃
Think of a race. Some colors run faster than others. Each substance has a unique Rf value under fixed conditions — like a fingerprint for separation.
The Formula
RETENTION FACTOR
Rf = d_component / d_solvent

🔬 Why We Collect Data
Now we need evidence — real numbers!
Parameters alone are just words. To teach an AI, we need measurements. Each data point is a trial: "with these settings, the swing took this long."
Your job: Adjust the sliders and record swing times. The more varied your data, the smarter the AI becomes.
🤖 After Data Collection
Then the AI takes over!
Once you have enough measurements, you'll pick an AI model to train. It will study your data and try to discover the hidden formula on its own — no textbook allowed!
The big reveal: We'll then compare what the AI found vs. what physics textbooks say. Spoiler: they should match beautifully. 🎯
