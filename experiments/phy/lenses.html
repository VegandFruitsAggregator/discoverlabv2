The lens — 1000 years of bending light.
From Ibn al-Haytham's glass sphere to Newton's prism to Gauss's elegant equation, the convex lens has shaped astronomy, medicine and photography. One formula unifies it all.
🔭 Galileo used a convex lens in 1609 to build a telescope that revealed Jupiter's moons for the first time.
👁️ Your eye lens adjusts its focal length to focus near and far — a feat no man-made lens can yet match.
📷 Every camera, projector and microscope relies on the thin lens equation 1/f = 1/v − 1/u.

The Thin Lens Equation
IMAGE DISTANCE FROM LENS
1/f = 1/v − 1/u
f = focal lengthv = image distanceu = object distance
💡
A 20 cm focal-length lens with an object at 60 cm forms a real image at exactly 30 cm. The magnification is −0.5 — half-size and inverted. This is how cameras work!
