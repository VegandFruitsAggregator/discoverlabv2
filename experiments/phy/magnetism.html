🔬 This Experiment
What we are doing: Placing magnets near iron filings and measuring field strength.
Our aim: Find out what controls the magnetic field strength B.
What we will find: A formula that predicts B from magnet strength and distance.
💡
Think of a light bulb and a lamp shade. The closer you hold the bulb, the brighter the light feels. Move it far away and the effect drops quickly. A magnetic field works exactly like this — strong up close, fading fast with distance.
The Formula
MAGNETIC FIELD STRENGTH
B = (M / (d+1)) × (F/100)
B = field strengthM = magnet strengthd = distance (cm)F = filings density (%)
🌕
Earth itself is a giant magnet! Its iron core generates a magnetic field that stretches thousands of kilometres into space, protecting us from solar radiation. Birds and sea turtles use it to navigate.
Now it is your turn. In the next steps you will work as a scientist — collecting real measurements, training an AI model, and comparing what the AI finds to what the formula predicts.
