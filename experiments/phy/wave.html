When ripples meet, patterns emerge.
Drop water at two points and watch the ripples interact. Some regions rise higher while others flatten. This is interference — waves combining in space.
🌊 Waves can amplify or cancel depending on how they meet.
🎵 The same thing happens with sound waves — noise-cancelling headphones use this!
💡 Light also forms interference patterns — that's how soap bubbles get their colours.

🔬 This Experiment
What we are doing: Sending two waves from separate sources and measuring the resultant amplitude.
Our aim: Find out what controls the resultant amplitude at any point in space.
What we will find: A formula that predicts the peak amplitude from A₁, A₂, and phase difference φ.
🌊
Think of two ripples meeting on a pond. Where both crests arrive together the water rises high — constructive interference. Where a crest meets a trough, they cancel — destructive interference. The same happens with sound and light.
The Formula
RESULTANT AMPLITUDE
A = A₁ + A₂
A = resultant amplitudeA₁ = amplitude 1A₂ = amplitude 2
🎧
Noise-cancelling headphones generate a wave that is perfectly out of phase with ambient noise — the two cancel to near silence. This is destructive interference in your ears.

You explored wave interference!

🌊 Key Insight
Waves combine, not collide.
When two waves meet, they add up — that's superposition. The result depends on how aligned the waves are, not on their energy alone.
Your job: Adjust amplitudes and phase, record resultant amplitudes. Varied data teaches the AI the full pattern.
