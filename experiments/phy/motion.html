🔬 This Experiment
What we are doing: Combining forward and sideways velocities and measuring the resultant speed.
Our aim: Understand how motion in two directions produces a diagonal resultant path.
What we will find: The Pythagorean formula v = √(vₓ² + vᵧ²) predicts resultant speed precisely.
🚢
Like walking forward while being pushed sideways. A boat crossing a river moves forward under its own engine, but the current pushes it sideways. Both motions happen at the same time — the actual path is the combination.
The Formula
RESULTANT SPEED FROM TWO COMPONENTS
v = √(vₓ² + vᵧ²)
v = resultant (m/s)vₓ = forward (m/s)vᵧ = sideways (m/s)
✈️
Pilots constantly adjust for crosswinds using vector addition. A plane flying at 200 m/s into a 50 m/s crosswind must angle into the wind to fly straight — the resultant speed is still ~206 m/s!
