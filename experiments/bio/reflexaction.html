🔬 This Experiment
What we are doing: Measuring how long it takes a signal to travel through a chain of neurons.
Our aim: Find out what controls signal transmission time τ.
What we will find: A formula: τ = (L / v·M) + d·N that predicts timing with great accuracy.
🏃
Think of a relay race. Each neuron is a runner passing a baton. The faster they run (signal speed) and the shorter the track (axon length), the quicker the message arrives. Each handoff (synapse) adds a small fixed delay.
The Formula
TOTAL SIGNAL TRANSMISSION TIME
τ = (L / v·M) + d·N
τ = time (ms)L = axon length (cm)v = speed (m/s)M = myelination (0–1)d = synaptic delay (ms)N = number of neurons
⚡
The fastest nerve fibres conduct at 120 m/s — as fast as a racing car. Myelination (the fatty sheath around axons) is the key: it makes signals jump between nodes, multiplying speed dramatically.
Now it is your turn. In the next steps you will work as a neuroscientist — collecting real timing measurements, training an AI model, and comparing what the AI finds to what the formula predicts.

Now we need evidence — real numbers!
Parameters alone are just words. To teach an AI, we need measurements. Each data point is a trial: "with these settings, the nerve impulse arrived in X milliseconds."
Your job: Adjust the sliders and record transmission times. The more varied your data, the better the AI learns.
